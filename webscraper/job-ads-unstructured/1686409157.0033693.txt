The Fraunhofer Institute for High-Speed Dynamics, Ernst-Mach-Institut, EMI has a vacancy in Efringen-Kirchen for a student assistant position (m/f/d) for the processing of "Global Navigation Satellite Systems" (GNSS) chips in the department of Measurement and Sensor Technology.




What you will do

The department uses global navigation satellite systems (GNSS) for positioning and time referencing. We process and correct the acquired data by different means.




Your tasks:
Support the project team implementing new server-based post processing methods.




What you bring to the table

Degree in computer science, physics, mathematics or other engineering sciences or related STEM disciplines
Programming skills (ideally in Java/ Kotlin)
Expertise with RINEX file format is beneficial, but not mandatory
Independent work, initiative and self-motivation
Ability to work in a team
German or English language skills (German language skills are not mandatory)



What you can expect

Flexible working hours with the possibility of working from home (hardware provided)
Regular project meetings at the Efringen-Kirchen site to exchange experiences.
We are a modern, innovative research centre with a wide range of topics, a high level of practical experience and plenty of scope for your creativity and initiative.
You will work on research projects and benefit from our experience in project work with customers and partners from industry and public authorities.
We offer you an absolutely top working environment with excellent IT infrastructure, an interdisciplinary team of scientists and engineers and a very good working atmosphere.
Further work on the topic within the framework of an internship, bachelor's or master's thesis is possible.
