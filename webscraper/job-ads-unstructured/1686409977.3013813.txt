For the topic Research Data Management (RDM) at the IGB at the Berlin-Friedrichshagen location at lake Müggelsee, we are looking for a Research Data Management Officer to start as soon as possible.

In this position, you will support researchers in creating data management plans and publishing their data, and implement the service offerings in the area of data management, FAIR Data and Open Science.

You will work closely with the members of the RDM Competence and Technology Platform as well as the Information Management and Open Science Officer, and support the conceptual development and strategic management of RDM at the IGB. We offer you the opportunity to participate in national and international working groups.

Für den Bereich Forschungsdatenmanagement (FDM) des IGB am Standort Berlin-Friedrichshagen am Müggelsee suchen wir zum nächstmöglichen Zeitpunkt eine*n Referent*in für Forschungsdatenmanagement.

In dieser Position beraten Sie die Forschenden beim Erstellen von Datenmanagementplänen und bei der Publikation ihrer Daten und setzen die Serviceangebote im Bereich Datenmanagement, FAIR Data und Open Science um.

Sie arbeiten eng mit den Mitgliedern der Kompetenzplattform FDM und der Referent*in für Informationsmanagement und Open Science zusammen, und unterstützen die konzeptionelle Weiterentwicklung und strategische Steuerung des FDM am IGB. Wir bieten Ihnen die Möglichkeit, sich an nationalen und internationalen Arbeitskreisen zu beteiligen.

Your tasks
Advise and support researchers on RDM with a focus on data management plans, metadata schemas, and publication of research data according to FAIR principles
Raising researchers' awareness of sustainable data management according to good scientific practice
Development and implementation of workshops on the subject of RDM, including the development of information materials
Further development of the area of Research Data Management in close cooperation with the members of the RDM competence platform
Support in the implementation and updating of the IGB Data Policy
Development and streamlining of internal communication and networking structures within the institute, in particular with data managers of the research groups
Your profile
Completed academic university studies (master's degree, diploma or equivalent) in a field relevant to the job
Advantageous: Doctorate in a scientific field related to the IGB and/or additional qualification in the field of data/information management
Very good knowledge of scientific data management and current developments in open data, open science, and reproducible research
Hands-on experience working with data management plans and research data management; experience in conducting training is advantageous
Experience with digital research data and processes and related tools such as repositories, metadata schemas, Git and Relational Database Management Systems
Excellent communication skills in English; advantageous: knowledge of German
An outgoing personality with strong communication skills who enjoys service-oriented work and is willing to actively approach and support colleagues in RDM.
Our offer

We offer an exciting and diverse job at an international and dynamic scientific institute. In an environment that encourages and values active collaboration and new ideas, you will work with colleagues from a wide range of scientific disciplines and help organize a large spectrum of different data types. In close collaboration with the RDM competence platform, you will play a central role in helping to shape RDM at the IGB.

“Research for the future of our freshwaters” is the mission of the Leibniz Institute of Freshwater Ecology and Inland Fisheries (IGB). The IGB is Germany’s largest and one of the leading international research centres for freshwaters. We seek to understand the fundamental processes governing freshwaters and their communities. Our research findings help to tackle global environmental changes and to develop measures for sustainable water management. The IGB is a diverse and inspiring place to work and conduct research. We promote individual development at every career level and stand for lively exchange and cooperation. With more than 350 employees and guests from all over the world, we conduct research at five locations in Berlin and at Lake Stechlin (Brandenburg). IGB closely collaborates with numerous national and international universities and other partners in science and society and is a member of the Leibniz Association, which connects 96 independent public research institutes in Germany. www.igb-berlin.de

„Forschen für die Zukunft unserer Gewässer“ ist der Leitspruch des Leibniz-Instituts für Gewässerökologie und Binnenfischerei (IGB). Das IGB ist das bundesweit größte und eines der international führenden Forschungszentren für Binnengewässer. Wir möchten die grundlegenden Prozesse in Gewässern und deren Lebensgemeinschaften verstehen. Unser Forschungswissen hilft, den globalen Umweltveränderungen zu begegnen und Maßnahmen für ein nachhaltiges Gewässermanagement zu entwickeln. Das IGB ist ein vielfältiger und inspirierender Ort zum Forschen und Arbeiten. Wir fördern die individuelle Weiterentwicklung auf jeder Karrierestufe und stehen für lebendigen Austausch und Zusammenarbeit. Mit über 350 Beschäftigten und Gästen aus aller Welt forschen wir an fünf Standorten in Berlin und am Stechlinsee (Brandenburg) und sind eng mit nationalen und internationalen Universitäten und Partnern vernetzt. Wir sind Teil der Leibniz-Gemeinschaft, die deutschlandweit 96 eigenständige öffentliche Forschungsinstitute aus vielen verschiedenen Disziplinen vereint. www.igb-berlin.de
